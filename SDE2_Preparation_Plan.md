**How to solve each problem:**
1. Read problem → think for 10 mins without hints
2. If stuck, read only the approach (not code) → try again for 15 mins
3. If still stuck, read solution → understand → close it → code from scratch
4. After solving, optimize and analyze time/space complexity
5. Revisit after 3 days (spaced repetition)

## Phase 4: Interview Ready (Week 13-16)

### Week 13-14: Mock Interviews & Revision

**DSA:**
- Do 2 timed mock interviews per week (use Pramp/interviewing.io or peers)
- Solve 3-4 random LeetCode mediums daily under timer
- Revise all patterns - make a 1-page cheat sheet per topic

**System Design:**
- Practice 2 full system designs per week (45 min each, timed)
- Record yourself explaining - review for clarity
- Prepare to justify every design decision

### Week 15-16: Behavioral + Final Prep

**Behavioral Preparation (STAR Format):**

Prepare 6-7 stories covering:
1. **Ownership**: A time you went beyond your role
2. **Conflict**: Disagreement with a teammate/manager
3. **Ambiguity**: Made progress despite unclear requirements
4. **Impact**: Biggest technical impact you delivered
5. **Failure**: Something that went wrong, what you learned
6. **Mentoring**: Helped someone grow
7. **Tight deadline**: Delivered under pressure

**For each story, structure as:**
- **Situation** (2-3 lines): Context
- **Task** (1-2 lines): Your specific responsibility
- **Action** (4-5 lines): What YOU did (use "I", not "we")
- **Result** (2-3 lines): Quantifiable outcome

**Current Work Projects:**
- List all major projects (3-4)
- For each: architecture decisions, tradeoffs, scale, your specific contribution
- Be ready to go deep on any one of them for 20+ minutes

### Networking (1 hr/day, Fri)

**Resources:**
- Quantify everything in your resume (reduced latency by X%, handled Y requests)
- Keep resume to 1 page
- Apply through referrals (LinkedIn connections, college alumni)
- Apply to 5-7 companies per week starting Week 10

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Solving too many easy problems** - Move to mediums quickly
2. **Spending 2+ hrs on one problem** - Read approach after 20-30 min, code yourself
3. **Ignoring system design** - It's the differentiator for SDE-2
4. **Not practicing under time pressure** - Always use a timer
5. **Skipping behavioral prep** - Many rejections happen here at SDE-2 level
6. **Applying too late** - Start applying by Week 10, don't wait to "feel ready"
7. **Not taking notes** - Maintain a "patterns notebook" for revision before interviews

## Negotiation Tips (When You Get Offers)

- Always have 2-3 offers in hand before negotiating
- Never reveal your current CTC first; say "I'm targeting X based on market rate"
- Use competing offers as leverage: "Company X is offering Y, can you match?"
- Negotiate on total comp (base + stocks + bonus + joining bonus)
- 20 LPA is very achievable; many SDE-2 roles pay 25-40 LPA for 3 YOE at good companies
